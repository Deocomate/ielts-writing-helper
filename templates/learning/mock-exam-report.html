Bài viết của bạn (có chú thích)

The line graph shows CO₂ emissions per capita in five European countries from 1990 to 2020. Overall, all nations experienced a downward trend, with Sweden consistently having the lowest figures.

In 1990, the UK recorded approximately 10 tonnes per capita, the highest in the group. Germany followed closely at 9.5 tonnes, while France and Italy were both around 7 tonnes. Sweden rose up to only 6 tonnes, suggesting it was already the cleanest emitter.

By 2020, all countries had reduced their output significantly. Germany showed the most dramatic fall — dropping by roughly 5 tonnes. The UK declined to around 5 tonnes. France, Italy and Sweden furthermore recorded lower figures at approximately 4–5 tonnes each. The overall range narrowed considerable, reflecting widespread policy success.
